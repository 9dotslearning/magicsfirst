About

I was, for 25 years, a systems manager who wished, mostly vainly, to sculpt technology so it fit human hands. The last 10 years of that time I was posted at an elementary school. This was a lucky break, since I had long since, in a hopeful and idealistic moment, acquired a masters degree in education from Harvard that was otherwise completely useless. At the elementary school, I squirreled away a little time each week to teach students in grades 2 through 9 about digital citizenship, troubleshooting, typing, and — most joyfully — about coding.

And then I retired. But in a coincident twist of fate, my local school system launched a middle school computer science program. Since teaching computer science was the most joyful thing I knew, I promptly unretired. After that, I hung out with a lively cohort of teens and tweens in a classroom of my own, making art from robots, 3D websites about the kind of world we'd like to see, interactive stories that paint scenes with words, and all the usual other suspects like Scratch, Python, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. Most recently I've been teaching kids to make art with p5.js at Outschool, where young learners from around the world can take classes in almost anything.

magiCSfirst is the distillation of what those students taught me: in the best learning, the magic's first. When students find the magic, they teach you what they need to know.

Terry
