The proprietor of magiCSfirst is Terry Dash. She did straight time as an information technology manager for 25 years, with the last 10 years happily managing systems for an elementary school. This was a lucky break, since she had long since, in a hopeful and youthful moment, acquired a masters degree in education from Harvard that was otherwise completely useless. At the elementary school, she rescued a precious few moments each week from insatiable demands of hardware and software systems to teach students in grades 2 through 9 about digital citizenship, troubleshooting, typing, and — most joyfully — about coding.

And then she retired. But in a completely unexpected twist of fate, her local school system almost simultaneously launched its first middle school computer science classes. Since computer science is the most joyful thing she knows, and teaching is the second most joyful thing, she unretired. For the next 8 years, she taught Scratch, Python, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Twine, and robotics, and she hung out with a lively cohort of tweens in a classroom of her own.

magiCSfirst is the distillation of what those students taught her. The best learning starts with a little magic. Once students feel the magic, they teach you what they need to know.
